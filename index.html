Metal Jewellery

For Indian Couture Week (ICW), Antevorta collaborated with renowned designer Amit Aggarwal, crafting a stunning collection of metal jewelry pieces that were uniquely designed and meticulously constructed to complement his vision. The jewelry reflected Antevorta’s philosophy of capturing evolution, eternity, and harmony through couture, where fashion becomes a form of art and storytelling. Each piece incorporated intricate details and expert craftsmanship, adding a dramatic flair to the runway.
Antevorta’s jewelry and headgear for ICW redefined traditional accessories, blending an unconventional sensibility with a visionary, otherworldly aesthetic. These pieces transformed the runway into a spectacle, where fashion transcended its conventional boundaries. The headgear and jewelry, adorned with intricate metalwork, introduced layers of opulence and sophistication, heightening the couture experience and reinforcing Antevorta’s bold, imaginative exploration of form and design. This collection commanded attention, with innovative accessories that seamlessly blended with Amit Aggarwal's couture designs, creating a striking visual impact. The handcrafted drip jewelry celebrated the interplay between fashion and art, with each piece telling a story of timeless elegance. Antevorta’s craftsmanship turned every runway moment into an unforgettable performance, making the ICW collection a testament to the future of couture.

My collaboration helped streamline the design process, bridging creative ideas with the functional aspects of couture creation. By assisting in these key areas, I contributed to the successful realization of garments that not only embodied innovation but also adhered to the craftsmanship and quality expected at Indian Couture Week. The design process for the ICW collection demanded not only artistic creativity but also precision in translating couture-level craftsmanship into wearable art. I was responsible for crafting designs that captured the opulence and innovation that are synonymous with couture, creating striking, dramatic pieces that were perfectly aligned with the grandeur of the event. For prêt collections, the challenge was to balance fashion-forward elements with functionality and market appeal, ensuring the designs were accessible while maintaining a strong sense of style and craftsmanship.
Additionally, my role extended to creating bespoke designs for high-profile celebrities, where the garments needed to stand out and reflect both the personality of the wearer and the luxury of the brand. In each case, I ensured that my sketches seamlessly merged creativity with practicality, anticipating the needs of both the runway and retail environments.
Working with a visionary like Amit Aggarwal required a blend of forward-thinking design, attention to intricate details, and a profound understanding of fashion storytelling. My designs contributed to a collection that embodied the brand’s commitment to sustainability, innovation, and high fashion, pushing boundaries in both menswear and women’s couture. The collaborative effort resulted in pieces that were not only visually stunning but also showcased technical prowess, setting a new standard for couture and ready-to-wear collections alike.

Mori

The Business of Global Fashion

The project aims to complexities of the contemporary fashion industry and the global supply chain. In this project, we learned how apparel projects are designed, developed, sourced, merchandised, manufactured, promoted, marketed and retailed from some international perspectives: taking into account the impact of issues such as the global economy, and political and cultural influences and sustainable and ethical practices.
The Mori brand came up with the concept of personifying nature from our garments. The brand aims to bring nature psychologically close to our consumers through our aesthetics even amidst the difficult times of covid 19 where people have to stay at home and tend to take no notice of nature.

Goddess Lakshmi

World of WearableArt (WOW) 2020

"Goddess Lakshmi" is one of the most popular goddesses of Hindu mythology and is known as the goddess of wealth and purity Lakshmi is associated with the lotus flower and is sometimes referred to simply as the Lotus Goddess. Its characteristics are a perfect analogy for the human condition: even when its roots are in the dirtiest waters, the Lotus produces the most beautiful flower.
Inspired by the carved border of Indian architecture, my idea is to depict its explosion of energy through a vigorous explosion of the lotus. Transforming pre-loved Indian silk saree into the soul of a unique lotus structure and as well as implementing a sustainable model which involves up-cycling throw-away sarees.
