GreenThreads

 /span>

Wake up call

Irusu: Industry Projects

This project sought to raise awareness about the environmental impact of plastic waste, particularly in marine ecosystems. Drawing inspiration from the ubiquitous plastic bag, the garments incorporated abstract plastic waste and plastic wrap, challenging consumers to reconsider their use of plastic. The project’s goal was to acknowledge the harm caused by plastic waste without casting blame.
By intentionally incorporating imperfection and breaking free from traditional design constraints, the garments aimed to inspire a shift towards a ‘No Plastic’ vision. Each garment was a bespoke creation, tailored to the individual client, with limited colour options. Through this project, I hoped to contribute to a growing movement towards sustainable fashion and a reduced reliance on plastic.

The Simplicity

DLF Emporio Design Awards 2018-19
Exhibitions: 'FINALISTS' Grandfinale Display at DLF Emporio Design Awards

“The Simplicity” was an innovative collection which challenged conventional design, with a vision of simplifying the complex world of fashion in a streamlined and memorable silhouette. The inspiration for this garment comes from Issey Miyake's groundbreaking method of pleating the fabric after it has been cut and sewn.
We explored the impact of a variety of factors, including the global economy, political and cultural influences, and sustainable and ethical practices. The Mori brand introduced the concept of personifying nature through our garments. Our aim was to bring nature psychologically closer to our consumers through our aesthetics, even during the challenging times of the COVID-19 pandemic when people were confined to their homes and may have been neglecting their connection with the natural world.

2019

GracefulRevival

 /span>

Goddess Lakshmi

World of WearableArt (WOW) 2020

Inspired by the Hindu goddess Lakshmi, known as the goddess of wealth and purity, this project sought to create a unique and sustainable fashion piece. Lakshmi is often associated with the lotus flower, a symbol of resilience and beauty. Drawing inspiration from the carved borders of Indian architecture, I envisioned a garment that would capture the explosive energy of the lotus flower.
By upcycling pre-loved Indian silk sarees, I transformed these discarded materials into the soul of a unique lotus structure, promoting a sustainable and ethical approach to fashion. This project aimed to celebrate the beauty and resilience of nature, while also contributing to a more sustainable fashion industry.

2019

TimelessEco

 /span>

Re-Wild

This project involved the design of a Korean-inspired garment, focusing on sustainability and luxury. By utilising recycled post-consumer waste and post-cutting scraps, I created a seasonless, luxurious garment designed for longevity. This concept aligned with the growing importance of sustainability in the apparel industry, a key concern of mine as a consumer- and environmentally-aware designer. As consumers become more conscious of their environmental impact, there continues to be an increase in demand for ethical and eco-friendly fashion. This project aimed to capture this need whilst also recognising the continuing need for quality and style - two elements which are often sacrificed to meet sustainability goals across the industry.
The garment was a bespoke piece, tailored to the individual client's specifications. This ensured a perfect fit and a truly unique garment. Whilst the colourways were limited, they were carefully chosen to complement the design and enhance its overall appeal to the Korean market. By combining innovative materials, sustainable practices, and quality craftsmanship, the end result offered a luxurious and conscious choice for the modern Korean consumer.
